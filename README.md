This thesis explores the relationship between **Emotional Intelligence (EI)** and **Sleep Quality** among university students using advanced **Machine Learning (ML)** techniques.

University students frequently experience academic pressure, emotional stress, and lifestyle changes that affect both their emotional well-being and sleep patterns. This study aims to uncover complex, non-linear relationships between emotional intelligence traits and various sleep quality indicators.

Unlike traditional statistical methods, this research integrates **Machine Learning models** with **Explainable AI (XAI)** techniques to generate interpretable and data-driven insights.
